MSc Dissertation Project

"Structural Elucidation of Organic Compounds Using NMR Spectroscopy"

Department of Chemistry, AMU  |  Student ID: 19-OCHM-027  |  2019–2021

The dissertation examined the use of NMR spectroscopy (¹H, ¹³C, and DEPT) as a primary tool for determining the structures of organic compounds. The work involved systematic interpretation of spectral data — chemical shifts, coupling constants, multiplicity patterns, and integration — to establish molecular frameworks and functional group assignments.

¹H NMR SpectroscopyInterpretation of proton NMR spectra including chemical shift analysis, integration patterns, coupling constants (J-values), and multiplicity determination.
¹³C NMR SpectroscopyAnalysis of carbon-13 spectra to determine carbon framework, identify functional groups, and establish molecular backbone structure.
DEPT TechniquesApplied DEPT-45, DEPT-90, DEPT-135 for unambiguous classification of carbon atoms (CH₃, CH₂, CH, and quaternary carbons).
Structure DeterminationElucidated complete molecular structures of diverse organic compounds through systematic spectral correlation and assignment.
